# Next steps — Colorado Environmental Data Hub

This is the human handoff memo for Colorado Environmental Data Hub, the shareable companion to the `ROADMAP.md` checklist. Read it to learn what's built, what to do next, and who owns it, without opening a tracker or a board. The assignable, checkbox version of everything below lives in `ROADMAP.md`; this page exists so a PI or stakeholder can stay oriented in a couple of minutes.

The Hub is scaffolded at level **L1** — the repository structure, the documentation, and a documented **56-source catalog** are in place, so you can clone the repo, find your way around from `README.md`, and start working against real data. On top of that foundation, the **first data-liberation pipeline is now built**: `pipelines/reservoir-storage/` (issue #9) liberates Colorado **reservoir storage** from two public APIs — CO DWR/CDSS and USBR Reclamation RISE — into a tidy, fully-historical CSV (118 major reservoirs plus ~17 RISE reservoirs, back to the 1960s–80s for the major ones), with per-extract provenance, documented cross-source caveats (vertical datum, capacity baseline), and a reconciliation spot-check. Northern Water turned out to publish only boundaries, so the reservoirs it operates are sourced from RISE instead. Each pipeline also ships a Harvard **Dataverse** deposit kit and runs on a **monthly CI refresh** — see the `Pipelines` wiki page and the pipeline's own `README.md` / `AGENTS.md`.

The top next actions, tracked as an assignable checklist in `ROADMAP.md` and as GitHub Issues, are: **(1) finish and publish reservoir storage (#9)** — resolve the last three RISE reservoirs (crystal / powell / taylor-park), fill the reconciliation spot-check totals, then make the first **human-reviewed Dataverse deposit** (publish v1.0; a deposit stops at a draft until a person confirms, because a DOI is permanent); **(2) stamp out the sibling water pipelines** — streamflow (#10) and snowpack (#11) reuse the reservoir pipeline's shape and inherit its CI + Dataverse pattern; **(3) harden the source catalog** — the two `good-first-issue` data-quality tasks (#2 verify the flagged `needs_followup` sources, #3 quarantine the spurious NREL `nlr.gov` claim) are intentionally left unassigned for incoming undergraduates to claim, followed by #4 (add the `match_hosts`/`match_keywords` citation-match fields); and **(4) the corpus pipeline (#5)** — the journalist→citation L2 build that `context/architecture.md` already specifies. Default ownership sits with **Brian Keegan (PI, @brianckeegan)** unless a row names someone else.

A few items are marked **blocking** in `ROADMAP.md`, and they gate real actions — relying on unverified sources, running Stage-A citation detection, publishing, or climbing to the next level — so resolve them, or explicitly accept the risk in writing, before doing the thing they guard. The future news corpus also carries blocking copyright/fair-use and journalist-privacy duties (see the roadmap), which must be satisfied before any article text or journalist records are ingested. Publishing data has the same discipline: the Dataverse deposit is **draft-only** in CI and is published only on explicit human review. Do not route around a blocking item quietly; raising it here is how a stakeholder learns the project is paused for a good reason.

To grow scope on purpose, re-run the `data-project` skill in this directory and ask for the next rung (for example, "take this to L2" for the corpus pipeline), or use `data-liberation` to build the next water pipeline; either adds only the new work and leaves what already works untouched. Note that the Hub itself stays **L1** — the data-liberation pipelines under `pipelines/<name>/` are self-contained sub-projects, not Hub level-climbs. Climbing is how scope grows deliberately instead of by accident, so prefer it to hand-adding heavyweight structure this level does not yet need.

These tasks are filed as GitHub Issues (#2–#11) and organized on the **Colorado Environmental Data Hub** Project board so the team can assign and track them in the open — see `PROJECT-MANAGEMENT.md` for how that mirror works and how to keep it in sync. The `ROADMAP.md` checklist stays the source of truth; GitHub is a projection of it, so edit the checklist and re-sync rather than letting the two drift.
