# Next steps — Colorado Environmental Data Hub

This is the human handoff memo for the Colorado Environmental Data Hub, the shareable companion to the `ROADMAP.md` checklist. Read it to learn what's built, what to do next, and who owns it, without opening a tracker or a board. The assignable, checkbox version of everything below lives in `ROADMAP.md`; this page exists so a PI or stakeholder can stay oriented in a couple of minutes.

The Hub is scaffolded at level **L1** and has **climbed to L4** (collaboration + responsible-data & accessibility). In place: the repository structure, the documentation, a **56-source catalog**, the first built **data-liberation pipeline** (`pipelines/reservoir-storage/`, issue #9 — CO DWR/CDSS + USBR Reclamation RISE, a tidy day-resolution CSV with full per-site history, monthly CI refresh, and a Harvard Dataverse deposit kit), and now the full **collaboration and responsible-data layer**: how people work here (`CONTRIBUTING`, `ROLES` + `.github/CODEOWNERS`, `CODE_OF_CONDUCT`, `GOVERNANCE`, `CHARTER`, `collaboration-protocol`), how the data's duties are met (`responsible-data-checklist`, `data-management-plan`, `INSTALLED-BASE`, the bulletproofing/quality/accessibility checklists, the `data-card`), and nested `.skills/` that give agents task-specific guidance. The corpus's copyright/fair-use and journalist-privacy obligations are now first-class checklists rather than roadmap prose — and the `GOVERNANCE.md` sensitive tier is deliberately provisioned-but-empty, because the data today is public.

**Why the climb now, and what it unblocks.** L4 was the right move because the project is at two thresholds the scaffolds guard: the **undergraduate research team is about to be onboarded** (L3 is what they onboard into — roles, review ownership, contribution workflow, conduct), and the **news corpus** — the flagship next build — carries duties that must exist *before* the data does. Climbing to L4 turned those duties into checklists with definitions of done.

The top next actions, tracked as an assignable checklist in `ROADMAP.md` and as GitHub Issues, are:

1. **Onboard the team and partners (L3).** Fill the `ROLES.md` gaps and replace the `@brianckeegan` placeholders in `.github/CODEOWNERS` with real team handles ([#31](https://github.com/CUPIDS-Lab/co-environmental-data/issues/31)); **name the CEJ subject-matter contact** in `collaboration-protocol.md` ([#32](https://github.com/CUPIDS-Lab/co-environmental-data/issues/32), blocking before any citation coding); and **secure a CU Libraries / University Counsel review** of the fair-use/TDM posture and licensed-database terms ([#33](https://github.com/CUPIDS-Lab/co-environmental-data/issues/33), blocking before ingesting article text or library exports).
2. **Satisfy the corpus's responsible-data gates (L4).** Fill the **codebook** and run **inter-coder calibration** to clear Krippendorff's α ≥ 0.80 ([#34](https://github.com/CUPIDS-Lab/co-environmental-data/issues/34), blocking before coding); **validate the LLM citation detector** to precision ≥ 0.90 with quote-span verification before any label is treated as authoritative ([#35](https://github.com/CUPIDS-Lab/co-environmental-data/issues/35)).
3. **Publish reservoir storage (#9 last mile).** Run the `data-bulletproofing-checklist` + `responsible-data-checklist` and make the first **human-reviewed Dataverse deposit** (publish v1.0; a deposit stops at a draft until a person confirms, because a DOI is permanent) ([#36](https://github.com/CUPIDS-Lab/co-environmental-data/issues/36)).
4. **Keep hardening the catalog and stamping out water pipelines.** The two `good-first-issue` tasks ([#2](https://github.com/CUPIDS-Lab/co-environmental-data/issues/2), [#3](https://github.com/CUPIDS-Lab/co-environmental-data/issues/3)) are left unassigned for incoming undergraduates; streamflow ([#10](https://github.com/CUPIDS-Lab/co-environmental-data/issues/10)) and snowpack ([#11](https://github.com/CUPIDS-Lab/co-environmental-data/issues/11)) reuse the reservoir pipeline's shape.

Default ownership sits with **Brian Keegan (PI, @brianckeegan)** unless a row names someone else.

Several items are marked **blocking** in `ROADMAP.md` (#32–#36), and they gate real actions — onboarding into unowned roles, coding citations, promoting LLM labels, ingesting article text, or publishing — so resolve them, or explicitly accept the risk in writing, before doing the thing they guard. Publishing data has the same discipline: the Dataverse deposit is **draft-only** in CI and is published only on explicit human review. Do not route around a blocking item quietly; raising it here is how a stakeholder learns the project is paused for a good reason.

To grow scope on purpose, re-run the `data-project` skill in this directory and ask for the next rung — **L5 (publish as open knowledge):** the OKF `knowledge/` bundle, the public Datasette + Quarto site, agent-discovery (`ai-catalog.json`), and licensing notes. Climb after the pilot clears its exit thresholds. The `ROADMAP.md` checklist stays the source of truth; GitHub is a projection of it, so edit the checklist and re-sync (`.github/seed-github.sh`) rather than letting the two drift.
