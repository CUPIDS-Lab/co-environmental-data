# Code of Conduct — Colorado Environmental Data Hub

This project is part of CUPIDS Lab (University of Colorado Public Interest Data Science Lab), and we want it to be a place where contributors, collaborating organizations, and participants are treated with respect. This code adapts the [Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/) to our public-interest work; it applies to everyone who takes part — the undergraduate research team, CEJ collaborators, outside contributors, and maintainers — and we hold ourselves to it because the quality of the collaboration depends on it.

## Our pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers pledge to make participation in the Colorado Environmental Data Hub a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation. We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include showing empathy and kindness toward others, respecting differing opinions and experiences, giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback, taking responsibility and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes and learning from them, and focusing on what is best for the community and the people the work serves. Examples of unacceptable behavior include the use of sexualized language or imagery and unwelcome sexual attention or advances, trolling or insulting and derogatory comments, public or private harassment, publishing others' private information (such as a physical or email address) without explicit permission, and other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

Because this project measures the work of named journalists, we add one project-specific standard: treat the people in our data with the same respect we extend to contributors. The database records *whether* a source was cited, not quality judgments about a reporter; do not use the project's data or spaces to disparage, harass, or single out the journalists whose work we study.

## Scope

This code applies within all project spaces — the repository, issues and pull requests, the wiki, chat and meetings, and shared data systems — and also applies when an individual is officially representing the project or CUPIDS Lab in public spaces. Because our work touches contributors, participants, journalists, and collaborating organizations (CEJ, CU Libraries) who are not on the team, we extend the same care to interactions with them.

## Enforcement responsibilities

The project maintainers, working with the mediator and project manager named in `ROLES.md`, are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful. They have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this code, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it to accounts@brianckeegan.com (CUPIDS Lab). All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly, and all maintainers are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter of any incident. We will not retaliate against anyone who reports a concern in good faith. Concerns about how the project represents a journalist's work follow the correction / right-to-respond pathway in `GOVERNANCE.md`.

## Enforcement guidelines

Maintainers will follow these community-impact guidelines in determining the consequences for any action they deem in violation of this code. A **correction** addresses a minor or unintentional violation with a private, written warning and clarity about why the behavior was inappropriate. A **warning** follows a violation through a single incident or series of actions and includes consequences for continued behavior. A **temporary ban** follows a serious violation, including sustained inappropriate behavior, and removes interaction with the community for a specified period. A **permanent ban** follows a pattern of violation, harassment, or aggression toward individuals or groups.
